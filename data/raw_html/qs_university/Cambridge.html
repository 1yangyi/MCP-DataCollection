Features - Ancient Egyptian handprint discovered on 'soul house'
A 4,000-year-old handprint has been discovered on an ancient Egyptian tomb, in a discovery that gives a rare glimpse of a human touch from the distant past.

Features - Mice are vital in the search for effective new dementia treatments
Cambridge researchers are leading drug discovery to develop safer, more effective treatments for the millions of people affected by Alzheimer’s and other neurodegenerative diseases.

Year 10 students from around the Cambridge region took part in a special programme designed to inspire the next generation of female mathematicians.

Concerts, lectures, sports events, and more

Family - Summer at the Museums: until 31 August
Enjoy a great value day out with your family at museums in Cambridgeshire. The packed calendar of family friendly events is designed to spark your imagination, explore big topics and help you enjoy time together.

Exhibitions - Cultured Canines at the Museum of Classical Archaeology: until Saturday 18 October
Cultured Canines: Evolution, Emotion, Imitation is an exhibition which explores the history and mythology of different dog breeds – via the style of ancient Greek pottery.

Exhibitions - Curious Cures: until Saturday 6 December
Health and disease in the medieval world – and how our ancestors sought to cure everything from infertility to constipation – are the focus of a major new exhibition at the University Library.
